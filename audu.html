AUDU

Our IAT387 Directed Studies project involved designing a specialized snorkel accessory for individuals with medical conditions resulting in deficiencies that affect the oral region of the face. Our team had interests and skills in visual, branding, interactive, user experience design and 3D modeling. Since our previous projects were usually dealt with in strict digital environments, we wanted this directed study project to explore the 3D design field (industrial design) in a customer-facing project.

Instructor: Kenneth Zupan

Team Members:
Rishabh Johri ( Researcher, Business Guru, Ideator, Prototype Finishing Lead )
Sarah Daniels ( Researcher, Visual Designer, Ideator, Prototype Assembly Lead)
Qiaohui Xu ( Ideator, 3D Modelling Lead, Prototype Cutting & Trimming Lead )

Research & Ideation

The motivation for this product came from an inquiry on the Neil Squire Society Community Board - a Canadian national non-profit organization that helps those with disabilities through advocacy. We came across an individual who wanted to find some way to "keep her snorkel in her mouth" as she 'tends to swallow a lot of water on days when she is weak".

We began by researching existing breathing apparatuses and their limitations. We also studied the medical conditions that require the use of breathing apparatuses and how they affect the patient's daily life. Based on our research, we brainstormed ideas for a new breathing apparatus that would address the limitations of existing devices. We sketched out several designs and discussed their feasibility.

3D Modeling & Physical Prototyping

Once we had a design concept, we started 3D modeling the breathing apparatus in software including Blender and Ultimaker Cura. We iterated on the design several times until we were satisfied with the form and function.

After the digital design was complete, we used 3D printing to create physical prototypes of the breathing apparatus. We tested the prototypes for usability and comfort, making adjustments to the design as necessary.

User Testing & Finalizing the Design

We conducted user testing with users who require breathing apparatuses to gather feedback on the design's functionality and comfort. Based on the feedback, we made further adjustments to the design.

Once we had a design that satisfied the user's needs, we finalized the design and created detailed 3D models and technical drawings for manufacturing.

Reflection

Attention to detail is crucial: Creating a highly detailed and accurate 3D model requires a lot of attention to detail. From gathering reference materials to optimizing the model for use in Twinmotion, every step of the process required a high level of precision and care.

Technical skills are important: The project required a range of technical skills, including proficiency in Blender and Twinmotion. While these skills can be challenging to learn, they are essential for creating high-quality 3D models.

Overall, I found the experience of creating a 3D model for Holland Park to be a valuable learning opportunity. The project challenged me to develop new technical skills. The combination of Blender and Twinmotion allowed me to create a highly detailed and visually stunning 3D model of Holland Park that would serve as an excellent showcase for student work.
