Holland Park

For IAT233 Spatial Design final project, I collaborated with Professor Kenneth Zupan on a individual 3D modeling project named Holland Park. The project involved creating a detailed model of the Holland Park using software Blender and Twinmotion. I explored the field of 3D design field (scene modeling) in this project. Students in IAT233 could quickly exhibit their pavilion work on my sand table model.

Research & Data Collection

At the suggestion of Professor Kenneth, I learned more about Holland Park and began to collect data about the park. Accurate dimensional scale data is necessary to build the scene modeling, which not only helps to ensure the realism and credibility of the scene, but also improves the efficiency. Therefore, I spent a lot of time and effort to find and organize this data to ensure that I could move forward smoothly in the scene modeling process.

The website https://cosmos.surrey.ca/external/, established by City of Surrey, provides a full range of data on Holland Park, including path, trees, buildings, roads, etc.

3D Modeling & UV Editing

Once we had a design concept, we started 3D modeling the breathing apparatus in software including Blender and Ultimaker Cura. We iterated on the design several times until we were satisfied with the form and function.

After the digital design was complete, we used 3D printing to create physical prototypes of the breathing apparatus. We tested the prototypes for usability and comfort, making adjustments to the design as necessary.

Scene Enrichment

We conducted user testing with users who require breathing apparatuses to gather feedback on the design's functionality and comfort. Based on the feedback, we made further adjustments to the design.

Once we had a design that satisfied the user's needs, we finalized the design and created detailed 3D models and technical drawings for manufacturing.

Our specialized breathing apparatus project showcases our ability to combine our skills in visual, interactive, and user experience design to create a product that addresses real-world problems. The project speaks to our ability to research, ideate, prototype, and iterate on a design until we achieve the desired outcome. My specific contribution to the project, including the digital 3D modeling and physical 3D printing of the prototypes, demonstrates my proficiency in 3D modeling and rapid prototyping techniques.
