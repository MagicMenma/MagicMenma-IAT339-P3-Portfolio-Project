The Sunside

The Sunside in Holland Park was the IAT233 Spatial Design final project involved designing a walkway canopy pavilion. By learning the propagation-based parametric systems, we applied and explored parametric modelling systems in the Rhino & Grasshopper. Working with parametric modeling systems was challenging at first, but their power and flexibility make them a powerful design medium. Thanks to these tools, we can efficient iteration and rapid prototyping, enabling designers to quickly refine and perfect our ideas. The Sunside is the idea we beautifully materialized with parametric modeling in the spatial design.

Instructor: Halil Erhan

Team Members:
Claire Yuan ( Researcher Lead, Ideator, Sketcher)
Kaixi Zhang ( Researcher, Ideator Lead )
Qiaohui Xu ( Visual Designer Lead, 3D Designer, Video Provider )

Research & Ideation

According to the request, our pavilion aims to create a multi-functional space in which people can walk freely, communicate alone or with friends, as well as enjoy the views in clear weather. During the design process, we paid special attention to providing ample space and opportunities for interaction between people.

Moreover, we recognized that the pavilion itself can be an interactive medium, so in order to integrate an abstract sculpture into the overall design of the pavilion, we specially designed a unique display space for it. We designed the entire pavilion as a fluid whole, and by raising and rotating the walls surrounding the abstract sculptures by 90 degrees, we achieved an arched top.

Study the Context

To ensure a comprehensive understanding of the pavilion's context, we analysis considered a wide range of factors to gain insights into the unique context of the pavilion within Holland Park. This involved examining its precise location, assessing the surrounding elements, built environments, access paths, traffic patterns, and noteworthy location features like landmarks, terrain characteristics, and orientation.

By leveraging the resources of the City of Surrey Mapping Online System, along with Google Earth's 3D data and conducting on-site field research, we conducted a detailed analysis of Holland Park. Through the combination of advanced mapping technologies and firsthand observations, we were able to carefully evaluate various aspects of the park and ultimately identify a well-suited location for our pavilion.

Finalization

We conducted user testing with users who require breathing apparatuses to gather feedback on the design's functionality and comfort. Based on the feedback, we made further adjustments to the design.

Once we had a design that satisfied the user's needs, we finalized the design and created detailed 3D models and technical drawings for manufacturing.

Reflection

Attention to detail is crucial: Creating a highly detailed and accurate 3D model requires a lot of attention to detail. From gathering reference materials to optimizing the model for use in Twinmotion, every step of the process required a high level of precision and care.

Technical skills are important: The project required a range of technical skills, including proficiency in Blender and Twinmotion. While these skills can be challenging to learn, they are essential for creating high-quality 3D models.

Overall, I found the experience of creating a 3D model for Holland Park to be a valuable learning opportunity. The project challenged me to develop new technical skills. The combination of Blender and Twinmotion allowed me to create a highly detailed and visually stunning 3D model of Holland Park that would serve as an excellent showcase for student work.
